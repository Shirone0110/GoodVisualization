What is a good Visualization?

Based on Alberto Cairo's The Truthful Art, there are 5 qualities that make up a good visualization:

  1. It is truthful
  2. It is functional
  3. It is beautiful
  4. It is insightful
  5. It is enlightening

In these following examples, we will discuss more about each of the quality.

Take a look at this global warming data visualization at Bloomberg Businessweek: What's warming the world?

Cool, isn't it? Now let's see why it's ...

  1. Truthful

    At the end of the website, there is a part What the Lines Show stated that data was taken from GISS's article. So we can tell that this information is official and trustworthy.

  2. Functional

    The data was presented by 2 lines at a time, comparing the change of one element or a group of element with the base change. The lines are place at the same position so that people can compare them easily.

  3. Beautiful

    The lines have different color for easy distinguish, with proper scale so that the line fluctuation is big and clear. The plot area is empty and white, the dot area near the line has a light color makes it more pleasing to look at.

  4. Insightful

    Have you ever think of other reasons for global warming rather than carbon dioxide and greenhouse effect? Well i haven't. These graphs show that there are also natural factors, and there is even factor that decrease the average temperature. And when we put all of them together, they match the observed temperature. This has also emphasize that the most important factor is still greenhouse gases.

  5. Enlightening

    Global warming has been one of the principal topic that humankind have to face, but many people still hasn't realize how much their actions are impacting on global warming. This visualization is also a warning towards everyone.
